Coffee Roasts & Brewing Methods

The flavor of a coffee bean depends heavily on how long it's roasted and how it's brewed.

Coffee Roasts

Roasting brings out the aroma and flavor locked inside the green coffee beans. The most common roasts are:

  • Light Roast: Toasted grain taste, with a pronounced acidity.
  • Medium Roast: Balanced flavor, aroma, and acidity.
  • Dark Roast: A bittersweet, smoky taste, with less acidity.

Brewing Methods

The method you use to brew your coffee impacts its final taste and strength. Here are a few popular ones:

  1. Pour-over: A manual method that highlights the coffee's subtle notes.
  2. French Press: A full-immersion method that results in a rich, full-bodied brew.
  3. Espresso: A concentrated shot made by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee.
